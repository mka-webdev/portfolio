# My Contribution

This file summarises my contribution to **Wisdom**, a collaborative Unity/C# 2D game project developed by **Team Mmm Mmm Jeans**.

Wisdom was a team-based university game development project. Although formal roles existed, the project was highly collaborative, with team members contributing across programming, design, documentation, art, sound, production, dialogue, mechanics, playtesting, and refinement.

My contribution focused on gameplay implementation, interaction design, playtesting, feedback analysis, and technical documentation.

---

## Contribution Overview

My work on Wisdom included:

* gameplay programming support
* C# scripting for interactive gameplay elements
* health potion interaction work
* puzzle and interaction design contribution
* inventory and board interaction design contribution
* playtesting strategy and coordination
* feedback collection and analysis
* feedback-driven iteration
* technical design documentation
* review and refinement of mechanics and player experience

---

## Gameplay Programming and Interaction Work

I contributed to the implementation and refinement of gameplay systems using Unity and C#.

My programming-related work included supporting interactive gameplay elements and helping connect game mechanics with player actions. This included work around puzzle interactions, board-based interactions, inventory-related interactions, and health potion functionality.

This contribution was not only about writing code, but also about checking how the implemented systems felt during play and whether the player could understand what to do.

---

## Puzzle, Board, and Inventory Interaction Design

A significant part of my contribution involved the design and refinement of player interactions.

I contributed to how puzzle boards, inventory elements, and related interaction systems worked from the player’s perspective. This included thinking through how players would collect, use, move, and understand interactive items during gameplay.

This work connected design and implementation, because the interaction systems needed to be clear enough for players to understand while still supporting the intended puzzle experience.

---

## Playtesting Strategy and Coordination

I developed and led the playtesting process for Wisdom.

This included planning how feedback would be collected, identifying what parts of the game needed to be tested, and helping the team understand where players were experiencing confusion or friction.

The playtesting process helped evaluate:

* whether players understood the puzzle interactions
* whether the game flow was clear
* whether interactive elements were noticeable
* whether the difficulty felt appropriate
* whether gameplay systems worked as intended
* whether changes improved the player experience

---

## Feedback Collection and Analysis

I gathered and analysed player feedback during the project.

This feedback helped identify usability issues, gameplay confusion, and areas where interaction design could be improved. The aim was not only to collect comments, but to understand what the feedback showed about the player experience.

The feedback analysis supported decisions around:

* improving puzzle clarity
* refining interaction cues
* adjusting gameplay balance
* improving player guidance
* making interactive elements easier to understand
* reviewing whether changes addressed earlier feedback

---

## Feedback-Driven Iteration

I contributed to iterative refinement of the game based on feedback.

This included reviewing playtesting results, identifying repeated issues, and helping guide changes that improved gameplay clarity and player experience.

The project used feedback as part of the development process rather than treating testing as something separate from implementation. This helped connect game design, programming, testing, and documentation into one delivery process.

---

## Technical Documentation

I contributed to the technical design documentation for Wisdom.

This included documenting gameplay systems, mechanics, interaction logic, and technical implementation details. The documentation helped explain how the game worked and provided supporting evidence for the project’s design and development decisions.

---

## Collaboration

Wisdom was a collaborative project, and many parts of the game were shaped by team discussion, shared problem solving, and cross-role contribution.

My contribution should be understood as part of a wider team effort. I was responsible for important gameplay, testing, feedback, and documentation work, but the final game was the result of combined contributions from the full team.
