# Playtesting and Feedback Summary

This file summarises the playtesting and feedback work completed for **Wisdom**, a collaborative Unity/C# 2D game project developed by **Team Mmm Mmm Jeans**.

The original Game Design Document is used as the source of truth for this summary, but it is not published in this repository. This file provides a portfolio-safe summary of the playtesting process, feedback analysis, and iteration work completed during the project.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of playtesting was to check whether players could understand, navigate, and complete key parts of the game.

The playtesting process focused on:

* gameplay clarity
* puzzle interaction
* inventory and item use
* level flow
* player guidance
* difficulty balance
* usability issues
* repeated points of confusion
* whether changes improved the player experience

This was important because Wisdom included puzzle-based interactions, dialogue, item collection, inventory use, and platformer gameplay. These systems needed to work technically, but they also needed to make sense to players.

---

## Playtesting Approach

The playtesting process was planned around gathering feedback from players and using that feedback to improve the game.

The process included:

* identifying parts of the game that needed testing
* observing how players interacted with puzzles and gameplay systems
* collecting feedback from playtesters
* reviewing repeated issues or patterns
* discussing feedback with the team
* adjusting gameplay, interaction cues, and puzzle clarity
* checking whether changes addressed earlier problems

Playtesting was treated as part of the development process rather than something done only at the end.

---

## Areas Tested

The main areas tested included:

### Puzzle Interaction

Players needed to understand how puzzle boards and related interactions worked. Testing helped identify whether the puzzle mechanics were clear, whether players understood what they were expected to do, and whether visual or interaction cues needed improvement.

### Inventory and Item Use

The game included item collection and interaction systems. Playtesting helped check whether players understood how collected items related to puzzles, progression, or gameplay.

### Game Flow

Testing helped evaluate whether players could move through the game in the intended order and understand where to go next.

### Player Guidance

Feedback helped identify where players needed clearer guidance, stronger cues, or better explanation of interactions.

### Difficulty and Balance

Playtesting helped check whether gameplay difficulty, damage, obstacles, and puzzle expectations felt appropriate.

### Usability and Clarity

The process also helped identify general usability problems, including confusing interactions, unclear objectives, or elements that players did not notice.

---

## Feedback Collection

Feedback was collected from players after testing the game.

The aim was not only to record opinions, but to understand what the feedback showed about the player experience. Feedback was reviewed for repeated patterns, especially where multiple players experienced similar confusion or difficulty.

Feedback was used to identify:

* unclear puzzle instructions
* confusing interaction points
* areas where players needed more guidance
* gameplay elements that were not noticeable enough
* difficulty or balance issues
* opportunities to improve flow and usability

---

## Feedback Analysis

After feedback was collected, it was reviewed and grouped into practical improvement areas.

The analysis focused on questions such as:

* What did players understand quickly?
* Where did players become confused?
* Which interactions were unclear?
* Were players able to complete the intended flow?
* Did the puzzle systems communicate enough information?
* Were repeated issues appearing across multiple players?
* Did the proposed changes directly address the problem?

This helped separate isolated opinions from issues that affected the game experience more broadly.

---

## Iteration and Improvements

Feedback was used to guide iteration during development.

Improvements included refining player guidance, adjusting interaction clarity, reviewing puzzle usability, and improving the way players understood gameplay systems.

The feedback process supported changes such as:

* making puzzle interactions easier to understand
* improving cues around interactive elements
* refining tooltips or guidance text
* adjusting gameplay balance
* improving player flow through the game
* reviewing whether earlier feedback had been addressed

This process helped the game improve through evidence rather than guesswork.
