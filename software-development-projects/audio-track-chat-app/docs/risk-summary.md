# Risk Summary

This document summarises the risk register maintained during the Audio Track Chat App team project.

The original risk register was updated weekly from Week 8 to Week 11. This public portfolio version combines those weekly updates into one cleaned summary. Individual names, private links, meeting recordings, and weekly Trello screenshots are omitted.

## Risk scale

- **Likelihood:** Low, Medium, High
- **Impact:** Low, Medium, High
- **Status:** Open, Mitigated, Closed

## Weekly risk movement

### Week 8

The initial risk register identified five project risks:

- R1: schedule flaws
- R2: incomplete specifications
- R3: poor productivity
- R4: scope creep
- R5: staff turnover / availability

At this stage, all risks were open.

R1 and R2 were reduced to low likelihood because the team was communicating, the workload appeared feasible, and the requirements were understood well enough to proceed. R3, R4, and R5 remained open as precautionary risks.

The team was geographically dispersed, so communication and meeting attendance were treated as important risk factors. Weekly meetings, shared documentation, and shared repository access were used to reduce coordination risk.

### Week 9

R3 was reduced from medium likelihood to low likelihood because all five team members were responsive and productive.

R1, R2, R4, and R5 remained open with no major changes. Requirements were considered clear, development was progressing within scope, and all team members attended the Week 9 meeting.

### Week 10

R5 increased from low likelihood to medium likelihood because availability became more sensitive as the team approached the final sprint.

A peer-coding session had to be rescheduled due to availability clashes. The issue was mitigated quickly because the team responded and adjusted the meeting time. However, the likelihood of R5 was raised to medium as a precaution because the project was moving closer to final submission.

R1, R2, R3, and R4 remained open with no major changes. The team understood the time constraints, clarified specifications during meetings, maintained productivity, and avoided adding unnecessary scope.

### Week 11

All risks were closed at the end of the project.

R1 temporarily increased to medium likelihood because of the volume of deliverables before final submission. It was later reduced back to low after the required technical draft was delivered on time.

R2 increased to medium likelihood because API integration with the application and user interface required review and adjustment. The project handout left some room for interpretation, so clarification and assumption management remained important until final delivery.

R3 stayed low because the team remained productive.

R4 stayed low because the team continued to work within the required scope and avoided gold-plating.

R5 was reduced back to low because all team members were actively working on the remaining tasks, and the team continued to show flexibility with availability and task coverage.

## Risk progression by item

### R1: Schedule flaws

Schedule risk remained important throughout the project because deliverables were tied to weekly milestones and final submission deadlines.

The risk was reduced early because the team maintained weekly sprint check-ins and communicated regularly. It temporarily increased in Week 11 due to the final volume of work, then returned to low likelihood after key draft work was completed on time.

Final status: **Closed**.

### R2: Incomplete specifications

Specification risk was tracked because the project brief required interpretation, especially around API integration and user interface behaviour.

The risk was reduced early after the team clarified assumptions and used tutor Q&A where needed. It increased again in Week 11 when API integration and GUI behaviour needed review and adjustment. The risk was closed after the project was finalised.

Final status: **Closed**.

### R3: Poor productivity

Productivity risk was included as a precaution because the project depended on multiple team members completing work across a short timeframe.

The likelihood was reduced to low in Week 9 because the team was responsive and productive. Pair programming and smaller task breakdowns were used as mitigation strategies.

Final status: **Closed**.

### R4: Scope creep

Scope creep risk was tracked because the project included multiple features such as chat, audio tracks, playlists, ratings, favourites, security, API integration, and testing evidence.

The team managed this risk by staying close to the required use cases and avoiding unnecessary gold-plating. The likelihood remained low throughout the project.

Final status: **Closed**.

### R5: Staff turnover / availability

Availability risk was important because the team was geographically dispersed and relied on meetings, shared documentation, and shared repository access.

The risk increased in Week 10 when a peer-coding session had to be rescheduled. It was mitigated quickly through team flexibility and communication. By Week 11, the likelihood was reduced again because all team members were actively working on remaining tasks.

Final status: **Closed**.
